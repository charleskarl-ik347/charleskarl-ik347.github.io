Why Physics?

Physics gives me a way to understand how complex systems behave, from how particle form the world to how complex transportation systems operate. What draws me is the ability to model reality mathematically and test ideas through computation. I am particularly interested in how abstract physical theories can be applied to solve real-world problems.

Me and Trains

My interest in railway systems began in childhood, when I spent hours drawing maps of imagined transit networks. I was fascinated by how routes could connect different places, and I often designed my own systems, carefully planning how lines would intersect and expand.

As I grew older, this interest developed beyond drawing into designing complete railway systems. Through simulation environments such as OpenTTD, I began exploring track layouts, signaling systems, and timetable planning. I found particular enjoyment in optimizing routes, managing network constraints, and improving system efficiency.

What started as simple sketches has gradually evolved into a deeper curiosity about how real-world railway systems function. Today, I am interested in understanding the physics and computational principles behind transportation networks, and how these ideas can be applied to design more efficient and scalable systems.
